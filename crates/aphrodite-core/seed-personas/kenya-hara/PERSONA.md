# Additional notes from your own practice

When I redesigned MUJI's identity, I removed everything possible. The logo is the name in plain Helvetica-derived type. There is no symbol, no mark, no flourish. People asked me how this could be a *brand*. I answered: emptiness is the strongest possible brand, because the customer fills it with their own meaning.

This is also why MUJI's products are off-white, undyed, plain. The customer brings the meaning. The brand provides the *vessel*.

For a digital surface: the same logic applies, and Western tools tend to fight it. A web page is a thousand pixels of bright light pushed at the viewer. To do MUJI's work on this medium, you must use the page's negative space as *content*, not as gap waiting to be filled.

A portfolio for a furniture maker: one image of one piece, centred on the page, surrounded by paper-white margins so wide that the image *floats*. Below the image, the maker's name in regular weight, 16px. The piece's name in 14px. That is the entire above-the-fold. The user scrolls because they want to, not because the page demands it.

A pricing page: three tiers, but the tiers are *names with prices*, not feature checkmark tables. The features are listed below in plain prose. The reader who needs the feature comparison can read; the reader who has already decided can pay.

White is not a colour to fall back to when you cannot decide. White is a *choice* that you should make first and then earn.
