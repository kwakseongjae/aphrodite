# Figma — why it matters

Figma's marketing site is the canonical case of "a design tool marketing to designers." It avoids the dev-tool dark-default register (Figma is light-default — designers work in light mode often) and the design-agency restraint register (Figma is *cheerful*, not austere). Threads a middle line: type-driven hero with one carefully-illustrated visual element, real product UI screenshots, a single warm-accent colour that signals creativity without joining the SaaS-gradient cliché.

## What to absorb

- **Light-default** for design-tool surfaces. Designers actually use light UI; matching the audience's editor mode is correct.
- **Single warm accent** — Figma's brand uses red-orange `#f24e1e`, purple `#a259ff`, green `#0acf83` as separate brand colours but never two at once on a single page. Pick one warm hue per page; let the rest stay neutral.
- **Product UI screenshots as content** — Figma's heroes show real canvas, real layers, real component props. Not floating glass cards. The UI IS the marketing.
- **One playful illustration per major section** — usually a Mariko Hirayama-adjacent style: flat, simple, friendly. Never overdrawn.
- **Type pairing: Inter or similar grotesque at all weights.** Light for body, semibold or bold for headlines. Figma's own type (Whyte) is paid; Inter is the closest free equivalent.
- **Generous section padding** — 160-200 px vertical between major sections. Marketing-funnel density is wrong for a product-led-marketing surface.

## What NOT to copy

- Figma's specific brand colours (`#f24e1e` warm red, `#a259ff` purple) are *theirs*. Pulling them produces "trying to look like Figma."
- The flat illustration style is a *specific* sub-style; if your brief doesn't include illustration commission budget, leave a placeholder, don't fake it.
- "Where teams design" register copy is theirs. Direct verbs, but find your own.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Hero structure: small eyebrow (11 px caps in accent) → 56-80 px headline → 18-20 px lede → primary + ghost CTA → product UI screenshot (full-bleed or 1200 px max)
- Section pattern: heading → 2-3 paragraph block → real product UI snippet → next heading
- Code-equivalent: a screenshot of a layer panel, a component panel, an inspect panel — design tools have UI panels that read like code blocks
- Footer: 4-column nav, 13 px regular, hairline divider above
- Avoid: "Sign up free" stickiness — let the CTA cool down to one ask
