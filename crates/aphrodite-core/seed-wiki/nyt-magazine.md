# NYT Magazine — why it matters

The NYT Magazine web reading experience is the most carefully-engineered serif-driven editorial site in English. The display family (Cheltenham, custom) and body family (Imperial, custom-licensed) carry a *specific* magazine register that has nothing to do with marketing or tech-Sunday-supplement. Each piece has a designed cover treatment (custom typography per feature) that breaks the system on purpose.

## What to absorb

- **Serif display at scale** — 60-96 px Cheltenham-equivalent (when not Cheltenham: Source Serif 4, Newsreader, Caslon Pro, GT Sectra). This is *journalism* register, not tech-startup register.
- **Body in 18-19 px** — *larger* than the web default 16 px. NYT determined that long-form readers want bigger body and looser line-height (1.5+). Editorial sites that default to 14-15 px body are mis-calibrating.
- **Photography that argues** — photo essays where the image is the story, not the decoration. When you can't commission, use *one* large photograph rather than three small ones.
- **Custom-typography hero per piece.** The most ambitious magazine sites break the system for feature heroes — hand-set type, custom illustrations, full-bleed art. The body reverts to the system. This treatment-then-system rhythm is what makes the magazine feel *curated*.
- **Byline as a primary element** — at the top, in a contrasting weight, with the writer's name and date. Not a footer afterthought.

## What NOT to copy

- The NYT's grey-and-black palette is *minimal* by design. Don't import this for a non-news context — it will read as "trying to look serious." Editorial pages outside news work better with one warm/cool counter-colour.
- Cheltenham is *trademarked*. The closest free serifs are Source Serif 4 (Adobe, MIT-equiv), GT Super (paid), and Newsreader (Google, free).
- Don't replicate the NYT's *full-bleed-hero-with-byline-overlay* pattern for editorial that isn't actually long-form journalism. It reads as posturing.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Article hero: title in Cheltenham 64-88 px, single-line preferred; byline in small caps 11 px below; dateline in 11 px italic
- Body measure: 540-680 px column width, never wider
- Drop cap: first letter only, 5-line height, in the display serif at body's typographic colour
- Paragraph spacing: indent-on-second-paragraph style (no blank line between paragraphs after the first) — this is *print* style; works on web with care
- Pull quote: 28-36 px display serif, centered, with 80 px vertical spacing above and below
