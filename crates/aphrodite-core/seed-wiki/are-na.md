# Are.na — why it matters

Are.na is a social bookmarking / research-organizing tool with a UI that takes restraint to its logical end. Every piece of content (text, image, link, file) is rendered as a uniform "block" — same width, same metadata strip, same hover affordance. The result is a UI that *feels infinite* and *doesn't preference any single format*.

## What to absorb

- **Uniform card primitive.** Heterogeneous content (image / text / link) all render as the same shape. This forces typography and image to carry the differentiation, not chrome.
- **Mono-tone palette: off-white background, near-black text, single accent (rust orange or muted teal depending on era).** No coloured tags. No coloured cards.
- **Type: a single sans family (currently Standard Book) at small sizes — 12-15 px body, rarely larger than 24 px even at display.**
- **Cards on a strict 4 or 6 column grid.** Inter-card gap 16-24 px. No card has decoration.
- **Search and tag interfaces are *plain text inputs* with a thin underline.** No "AI-powered" suggestion glow. The user is treated as competent.

## What NOT to copy

- Are.na's typography is *intentionally* small. A first-time user often complains it's too small. Don't copy the 12 px body for a portfolio aimed at a broader audience — the small type is part of Are.na's "we're a tool for people who read" register.
- The card grid is correct for *content that is heterogeneous*. For a portfolio of one maker's furniture, you usually want a magazine pacing (Apartamento) instead.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Card metadata strip: 11 px caps for the type (Image / Text / Link), 11 px lowercase for the title, 11 px italic for the source
- Hover state: card outline darkens 5%, nothing else moves
- Footer: single line, small caps, 11 px, with a manifest-of-values link rather than a generic "About"
