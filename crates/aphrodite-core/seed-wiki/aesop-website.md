# Aesop — why it matters

Aesop's web identity is the strongest contemporary example of *retail* surfaces designed with *editorial* sensibilities. Every product page reads like a slow-magazine spread: serif display at 60-80 px, body in 14-15 px Italic Garamond, photography hand-styled on warm-cream backdrops with a single brand-amber bottle. There is no urgency chrome. No "limited edition" countdown. No "20% off" banner. The customer arrives, reads, leaves. They come back because the page treats them as literate.

## What to absorb

- **Serif display at modest sizes.** Aesop's display family is Suisse Works (paid) — closest free pairs: Source Serif 4 or Crimson Pro at modest sizes (40-60 px, not 80+). This is *not* the NYT-magazine "screaming serif" register; it's the *paperback-classic* register.
- **Body in 14-15 px regular** — surprisingly small for the web default. The italic body for descriptions adds editorial inflection.
- **Photography hand-styled, single product per frame.** Warm-cream or olive-grey backdrops. The bottle is often placed *off-centre*, with one styling object (a folded cloth, a single olive leaf). The frame is 4:5 portrait.
- **Product copy is editorial.** Each product description is 60-100 words of prose, not bullet points. Ingredient lists are below the prose, in 12 px.
- **Type for product names is regular-weight, not bold.** A new arrival is "Geranium Leaf Body Cleanser" in 18 px regular italic — never "GERANIUM LEAF BODY CLEANSER" in caps bold.
- **One hairline divider** between sections — usually in `var(--colors-neutral-300)`, a warm-grey.

## What NOT to copy

- Aesop's specific palette (deep amber `#a76c47`, off-cream `#f0ead8`, deep-olive `#3c4030`) is theirs. The *register* (deep warm neutrals with a single saturation point) is transferable; the specific hex values are not.
- Aesop's photography is *commissioned* with specific stylists and lighting. Don't approximate with stock — leave intent-named placeholders.
- The cult-skincare brand voice ("an elixir of botanical extracts") only works for actual skincare. Don't lift the prose register for other categories.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Product hero: 4:5 product photo → 40-60 px display serif italic for product name → 60-100 word body prose in 14-15 px regular italic
- Hairline divider in warm-grey 1px
- Ingredient strip: 12 px caps regular weight, comma-separated, single line
- Body line-height: 1.65 (loose)
- Section vertical padding: 120-160 px
- Avoid: "Add to cart" with bright accent — Aesop's add-to-cart is the same dark text as body, with a thin underline
