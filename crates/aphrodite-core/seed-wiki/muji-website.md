# MUJI — why it matters

MUJI's web identity is the cleanest contemporary expression of Kenya Hara's "emptiness as receptivity" doctrine. The page is mostly off-white. Type sits in regular weight at 13–15 px body. There is no hero gradient, no decorative shape, no CTA button with an exclamation. The product is the content; the interface is the gallery wall.

## What to absorb

- **Negative space at 50–70% of canvas.** Not "more whitespace" — *intentional* whitespace, in measured intervals.
- **Off-white background** (`#f8f7f4` territory), not pure white. Cuts the screen's harshness without warming into parchment.
- **Single product photograph at viewport scale.** When you show one thing, show it big. When you show many things, show them at the same scale on a strict grid.
- **Plain-text contact / footer.** No social icons in marquee. The footer reads like a printed colophon.
- **Hairline dividers**, not card-and-shadow.

## What NOT to copy

MUJI is a retailer with photography of its own products. If your intent doesn't supply equivalent photography, *do not fake it* with stock — leave intent-named placeholders and let the user populate. The MUJI silence only works because the silence has something to point at.

Also: MUJI's product grid uses *its own* product photography style (white seamless background, neutral lighting). Copying the *grid layout* without the *photography style* produces a vacuum.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Top nav: small caps Latin + Hangul/kana at 12 px, generous letter-spacing
- Body line-height: 1.7 (loose for restrained registers)
- Product card: image-dominant, three-line text rail (name / category / price), no hover-flair
