# Linear — why it matters

Linear set the bar for B2B software marketing pages since ~2020. Their site is dark-default (the product is dark-default), uses a restricted palette of cold-violet accents on near-black, and treats product screenshots as the *content* — not decorative orbiting glass.

## What to absorb

- **Dark-default for dev-tool surfaces.** The audience runs IDEs in dark mode; meet them where they live. Light mode is the alt, not the canonical.
- **Cold-violet accent palette.** Linear's signature is `#5e6ad2` (their brand violet) on `#08090a` (near-black). Two primary colours, period. No gradient on the CTA.
- **Real product screenshots, sharp.** Not glass-morphed glowing fake-UI. The page shows what you'll see when you click "Get started."
- **Type pairing: Inter Display for headings, Inter for body.** Modern grotesque, no serif. Headings in 600–700 weight; body in 400.
- **Subtle motion.** Section transitions use small parallax and fade. No scroll-jacking, no full-screen video.
- **Code blocks** for keyboard shortcuts and CLI examples — a dev surface should look like a dev surface.

## What NOT to copy

- The cold-violet hue is *Linear's* brand. Don't pull #5e6ad2 wholesale; the *register* is what's transferable (cold accent, single-hue system, near-black base).
- Linear's marketing pages frequently use animated gradient meshes in the hero. Skip this — it's their signature, not a generic best practice. A fresh project copying gradient meshes reads as "trying to look like Linear."
- "Built for modern teams" copy: avoid. The copy you can lift is the *concrete-action* register ("Track issues. Build software faster.")

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Hero structure: 1-line eyebrow tag → 56-72 px headline → 18 px subheadline → CTA stack (primary + ghost) → product screenshot
- Section spacing: 160–240 px between major sections — generous, even for dense content
- Nav: 14 px nav links, generous letter-spacing 0.01em, no underlines, accent on hover
