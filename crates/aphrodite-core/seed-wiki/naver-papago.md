# Naver / Papago — why it matters

The dominant Korean web aesthetic — represented by Naver's portal, Coupang's commerce surfaces, and Kakao's app frames — sits at a *much higher information density* than Western equivalents. Western design canon often reads this density as "cluttered"; Korean users read it as *rich and complete*. If your brief is a Korean audience and you import a Western whitespace-heavy template, you may produce something that reads as *thin* or *underbuilt* to the actual readers.

## What to absorb

- **Higher information density when the audience is Korean.** Body text at 13-14 px is normal (vs 16-17 px Western default).
- **More content above the fold.** A Korean portal hero might pack 8-12 distinct content blocks; a Western hero would carry 2-3.
- **Tight grid gutters (8-12 px).** Western "breathing room" of 24-32 px gutters can read as "this is empty."
- **Colour-coded categories.** Korean retail / portal surfaces often colour-code sub-sections with low-saturation tints (mint, peach, lavender backgrounds for category headers).
- **Hangul at small sizes reads better than Latin at the same px.** A 12 px Hangul line is comfortable; a 12 px Latin line is straining. Don't apply Western "minimum 14 px" doctrine to a Korean surface.

## What NOT to copy

- Don't pull Naver's *actual visual chrome* (the green badge, the search bar style). That's their brand.
- This dense register is *not* universal in Korean design — high-craft / luxury Korean brands (CJ ENM, hyundai card) lean toward Western whitespace conventions. Use density only when the intent matches (consumer portal, marketplace, dashboard for Korean ops users).
- Don't import Korean density for a *Western* audience — they will perceive it as a regression.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Top nav: 8-12 links horizontally, all in 13 px Hangul (Pretendard or Sandoll), no caps
- Category headers: 14 px medium-weight, with a 4 px coloured underline rather than a card background
- Body: a 3-column grid at 1440 width, gutters 16 px, each column carrying a discrete content type
- Footer: dense, multi-column, with site-map style — not a "social-link + email" minimal footer
