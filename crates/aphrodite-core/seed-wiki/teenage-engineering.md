# Teenage Engineering — why it matters

Teenage Engineering's site is the high-water mark for "small hardware studio that wants the product to read like an architectural object." Each product (OP-1, OP-Z, PO-33, choir, computer-1) gets a page where the *physical product* is the page's primary visual element — usually photographed in a studio against a single coloured paper backdrop, often at unusual angle (3/4 from above, isometric-feeling). The hero photography does what the album cover does for Pitchfork.

## What to absorb

- **One coloured backdrop per product page.** TE often uses orange (#ff6900-ish), grey, off-white, or a saturated blue. The backdrop is *the brand* on that page; the product is the content. Pick a backdrop colour that the product photo argues with.
- **Type as labelling, not voice.** TE's body copy is laconic: "OP-1 field — synthesizer, sampler, sequencer, recorder. Six instruments. One device." Periods do the work paragraphs do elsewhere.
- **Single sans family** (their proprietary Teenage Engineering Display + Standard, fallbacks: GT America, Söhne, Inter). Display weight only for the product name itself; everything else regular.
- **Inline product specs.** A horizontal strip of small-cap labels: "DIMENSIONS · WEIGHT · I/O · BATTERY · PRICE". 11 px caps with thin underlines.
- **No CTA explosion.** A single "buy" button at the foot of each product page. No "Learn more" / "Subscribe" / "Get the deck" stack.

## What NOT to copy

- TE's orange (#ff6900-ish) is their brand. Picking orange for a product hero of a different brand reads as "trying to look like TE."
- The product photography is *commissioned* with extreme care. If you don't have studio-grade photography, leave intent-named placeholders; do not fake the TE look with stock.
- "OP-1" / "OP-Z" naming convention (codes plus version letters) is theirs. If your product has its own naming logic, use that — don't borrow OP-style codes.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Hero structure: large 4:5 portrait of the product on a single coloured backdrop (60% viewport height) → product name in 56-72 px → 2-line spec strip → buy button
- Spec strip: 5-7 labels in 11 px caps, joined by middots (·) or thin vertical bars
- Footer: single line, address + email + small social icons in mono-grey
- Body register: 14-15 px regular sans; lines short; periods carry rhythm
- Don't show: founders' faces, "our story" copy, marketing-funnel multi-section pages
