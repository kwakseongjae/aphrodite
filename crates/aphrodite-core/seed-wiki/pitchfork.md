# Pitchfork — why it matters

Pitchfork pioneered the "review with a decimal rating up top and a 600-word essay below" web format in the early 2000s. The current 2026 site has migrated to a Vogue-influenced editorial polish but retained the *rating-first* chrome (a 7.8 or a 9.4 sits in a circle at the top of every review). It's the strongest contemporary example of an editorial site whose *primary chrome* is a single high-contrast UI element repeated across every page.

## What to absorb

- **Rating-as-chrome.** The numerical score is set in a 96-120 px sans display, in a circle (or pill) at the top-right of the hero. It's the only ornamental element on the page; it earns its place by being the *headline metric*.
- **Album cover at full-bleed** — 1:1 ratio, taking 60-70% of the viewport vertically. Photography is *the album cover*, not a press shot. The release artwork carries the visual weight.
- **Display sans-serif** (currently Atlas Grotesk / Söhne Halbfett). Not a serif — Pitchfork chose sans because the editorial *voice* is contemporary-critical-essay, not literary-meditation. Match the family to the voice.
- **Reviewer byline + role** — "by [Reviewer Name], Contributing Editor". The role tag legitimizes the rating.
- **Tracklist as content.** A review page that *lists the songs* in 14 px monospace with timecodes is doing structural work, not decorating.

## What NOT to copy

- The rating-circle is a *Pitchfork* signature. Lifting it without the editorial reputation underneath produces "ranking-app vibes."
- Atlas Grotesk is paid; closest free pair is Inter at heavy weight (Inter 800).
- The full-bleed cover treatment works because every Pitchfork hero has an *actual* album cover to put there. If your editorial context doesn't have equivalent commissioned imagery, don't fake it.

## Reference fragments worth lifting

- Hero structure: album cover (1:1, full-bleed) → title in 56 px display sans → artist name in 24 px regular weight → rating circle 96 px top-right (often overlapping the cover by 20%)
- Review body: 18 px serif (Tiempos / Mercury / GT Super) — the *body* is serif even when the display is sans; rationale: long reading is easier in serif
- Pull-out specs strip: "Released 2026-03 · Self-released · 38:42" in 12 px monospace, below the headline
- "Best New Music" badge: a yellow square (#fde047 territory) with 11 px sans caps; if your editorial has tiers, a colour-coded square is more readable than text alone
