About

도현 장 has worked walnut in Seoul since 2018. The workshop sits in an industrial building near 성수동, close enough to the Han River that the humidity stays stable — a practical concern for wood movement.

Every log is sourced from the southern provinces, air-dried for eighteen months, then kiln-dried to 8% moisture content. No tropical hardwoods. No imported material that cannot be verified. The walnut is Korean-grown Juglans mandshurica when available, North American black walnut by commission.

Joinery is traditional: dovetails for drawers, mortise-and-tenon for frames, through-wedges where the joint should be visible. Surfaces are hand-planed and finished with polymerized linseed oil. No polyurethane. No lacquer. The wood breathes.

Lead time for new work is currently four to six months. Commissions begin with a conversation.
