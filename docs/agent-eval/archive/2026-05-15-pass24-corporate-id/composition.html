Form

Forma designs objects for manufacture — furniture, lighting, hardware, and industrial systems for the built environment.

Founded in 1961, the consultancy has maintained a continuous practice in mid-century industrial design principles: material honesty, structural clarity, and the moral obligation of designed objects.

Approach

Philosophy

Designed objects carry moral weight. A chair that cannot be repaired is a chair designed against the future. A lamp that obscures its own mechanism is a lamp designed against the hand that must maintain it. Forma's practice begins with these convictions and works outward toward form.

The studio maintains an archive of over 2,400 manufactured objects spanning six decades. Each project enters this lineage — not as nostalgia, but as accumulated structural knowledge. We know how a joint fails, how a finish wears, how a handle meets the palm after ten thousand grips.

Every Forma output is specified for manufacture. We do not produce concepts, mood boards, or design-direction documents. The deliverable is a production drawing set, a material specification, and a first-article inspection report. The object must exist.

  • Material-first specification
  • Structural honesty in assembly
  • Repairability as a design parameter
  • Anthropometric verification
  • Production drawing deliverables
  • First-article inspection protocol
